About

I am now a research assistant in the department of BioMedical Engineering (BME) at FMMU, under the supervisor of Prof. Xiuzhen Dong and Prof. Feng Fu. I am also a fifth-year Ph.D in the Science and Technology on Automatic Target Recognition Laboratory (ATR Lab) at NUDT, where I worked with Prof. Qiang Fu and Dr. Hongqi Fan in Adavance Radar Signal Processing (ARSP) group. My research interests lie broadly on biomedical signal processing, statistics, optimization, tracking and estimation.

Academia

My primary research is side-information assisted Compressive Sensing (CS). We (in collaborate with Dr. Zhilin Zhang) have developed advanced Bayesian learning algorithms, which are termed the BSBL framework. I used those algorithms in group target Direction-of-Arrival (DOA) estimation, radar imaging and dynamic scene tracking. We also applied CS as a low-complexity, low-power, robust data compression method in bio-signals (ECG, EEG, PPG, etc.) telemonitoring applications, a.k.a, Portable Healthcare. (it may also be called as eHealth or mHealth, however portable healthcare is IMHO future).

I am now building Electrical Impedance Tomograpgy (EIT) hardware/software at Dept. of Biomedical Enegineering, FMMU, under the supervisor of Prof. Xiuzhen Dong, Prof. Feng Fu and Prof. Xuetao Shi.

I am also a big electronic fan on amatuer radio (HackRF, GNURadio), homebrew PCB (Kicad, Cadsoft Eagle), embedded platforms (ARM cortex-M series, Xilinx FPGAs, and Labview) and Bluetooth technology (Roving Networks RN-42, Nordic nrf51822). An amatuer RADAR utilizing basic FMCW waveform is building on the way, with Dr. Hongqi Fan.
